Nostalgia

Intro

What is Nostalgia?

According to Arlin Cuncic of verywellmind.com; Nostalgia is a sentimentality for the past,typically for a period or place with happy personal associations. Nostalgia is usuallytriggered by something reminding an individual of an experience from the past. It is often characterized as a longing or desire to return to a former time or place.

There are 2 types of nostalgia:

• Positive nostalgia is characterized by happy, rose-tinted memories of the past. It is often associated with feelings of warmth, happiness, and comfort. (Arlin Cuncic)

• Negative nostalgia, on the other hand, is characterized by bittersweet or even painful memories of the past. It is often associated with longing, sadness, and regret. (Arlin Cuncic)

There are also 3 categories of nostalgia:

• Personal nostalgia is characterized by memories of specific people or events from one's own life. (Arlin Cuncic)

• Social nostalgia is characterized by memories of a time when one felt more connected to others. (Arlin Cuncic)

• Cultural nostalgia is characterized by memories of a time when one felt more connected to their culture. (Arlin Cuncic)

Historical Context

Nostalgia is a relatively new concept. The word was first coined in 1688 by Swiss physician Johannes Hofer, who defined it as a neurological illness of continually thinking about one's homeland and longing for return. (Arlin Cuncic)

Also, according to Melissa Kirsch of the New York Times, The word, roughly translated from the Greek, means “a longing to return home.”

Where to Find It

The most obvious place to find nostalgia is in your mind. Memories are a very strong source of nostalgia. Besides that, there is a wide variety of products are available nowadays that are meant to evoke nostalgia. According to Melissa Kirsch, Nostalgia is easily packaged and sold because it promises to create a community out of a cohort. In other words, an abundance of nostalgic products exist because there is a great demand for them. Another source of nostalgia is the internet. "The internet is an endlessly renewing nostalgia mine from which anyone can, at any time, extract a cultural gemstone — a music video from the early days of MTV, a jingle for a product long out of circulation — and post it for all to appreciate." (Melissa Kirsch) Of course, there are many other sources of nostalgia, but those are some of the most common places to find it.
